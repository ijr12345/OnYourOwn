Mental Health: Stress and Anxiety
Stress is a common aspect of life that everyone encounters at some point. It can be a fleeting moment or linger for days, impacting various aspects such as appetite, sleep, concentration, and social interactions. To prevent stress from becoming overwhelming, consider the following proven tips:

• Understand the root cause by pinpointing the key situation or feeling that is causing stress, as it is often a single issue among many.

• Practice stress-busting skills such as taking 10 minutes to chill out, meditate, count to 10 (or more), engage in physical activity, go for a walk, listen to music, or journal.

• Take action by addressing what you can change and talking to a trusted person, such as a school counselor, teacher, mentor, parent, or friend. Discussing the issue can provide clarity on how to handle it.

When stress escalates and transforms into overwhelming anxiety, characterized by a sense of impending danger or threat, it's essential to take proactive steps:

• Talk to someone trustworthy, like a school counselor, teacher, mentor, or parent, and share your feelings. Seek their thoughts and suggestions.

• Consult a doctor to rule out any physical factors contributing to your symptoms.

• Seek therapy by asking for a therapist referral, especially one trained to work with youth. Therapy can assist in processing feelings and developing strategies to manage stress and anxiety.

Remember, overcoming these challenges requires time and courage. Be patient with yourself, as progress may not happen overnight. Letting go of worries and fears while working through stress and anxiety will pave the way for more happiness and enjoyment in your life.
Maintaining Your Physical Health
For homeless teenagers, prioritizing physical health is a critical aspect of navigating life's challenges. The first step in this journey is securing a doctor who understands the unique circumstances faced by individuals experiencing homelessness. Fortunately, many communities have clinics or healthcare centers specifically designed to cater to the needs of those without stable housing. These facilities offer comprehensive medical services, ranging from routine check-ups to vaccinations, providing essential guidance on managing health concerns tailored to the challenges faced by homeless teens.

Emphasizing physical health is not only about addressing immediate medical needs but also establishing a foundation for long-term well-being. In addition to finding a doctor, it's important to explore preventive measures and health education. Many community health programs offer resources to help you adopt healthy habits, manage chronic conditions, and navigate the healthcare system more effectively.

If you're unsure where to begin, consider reaching out to local shelters, youth organizations, or social service agencies. These organizations often have connections to healthcare professionals who specialize in caring for homeless individuals. Taking control of your physical health is a proactive step towards building resilience and overcoming challenges. By accessing the available support in your community, you can establish a solid foundation for your well-being, ensuring that your physical health is a priority in your journey forward.
